🌐 Politeness in Practice: AI Dialogue Planner

Create and analyze AI-generated conversations through politeness theory

Step 1: Background Reading and Planning

📘 Start by reviewing key politeness theories:

  • Brown & Levinson (1987): Positive and negative politeness, face-threatening acts, and strategies.
  • Leech (1983): Politeness maxims such as tact, modesty, and agreement.
  • Spencer-Oatey (2008): Rapport management and intercultural considerations.

Then, choose 2–3 real-life scenarios where politeness plays a crucial role. Describe the participants, relationship, and goal of each interaction.

Examples:

  • A student requesting an extension from a professor
  • An employee giving feedback to their manager
  • Refusing an invitation from a friend without offending them

Step 2: AI Prompting and Dialogue Generation

💬 Use ChatGPT or a similar AI tool to generate conversations based on your scenarios. For each, create:

  • One version using a neutral prompt
  • One version asking the AI to be “extra polite”
  • One version asking for a more “direct” or “less polite” style

Prompt Example: “Write a conversation where an employee gives polite negative feedback to their manager.”

Step 3: Theoretical Mapping and Annotation

🧠 Analyze the AI-generated dialogues by identifying politeness strategies used in each line or turn.

Annotate: Label phrases as “positive politeness”, “negative politeness”, “bald on record”, or “off-record”.

Examples:

  • “I really appreciate your time...” → Positive politeness (showing approval)
  • “Sorry to bother you, but...” → Negative politeness (apology + mitigation)

Step 4: Analysis and Evaluation

📊 Evaluate the quality of the AI’s output. Use these criteria:

  • Strategy use: Did the AI apply politeness correctly?
  • Contextual fit: Was the level of politeness appropriate for the scenario?
  • Nuance: Did the dialogue reflect subtle language choices or just basic templates?
  • Consistency: Were politeness markers used throughout or just at the beginning?

Step 5: Write-Up and Reflection

📝 Write a report (1,500–2,000 words) including:

  • Introduction: Topic overview and aims
  • Methodology: Scenarios, prompts, and theories used
  • Data and Analysis: Annotated dialogue excerpts
  • Findings: What trends or issues did you observe?
  • Reflection: How competent is AI at pragmatic tasks like politeness?
